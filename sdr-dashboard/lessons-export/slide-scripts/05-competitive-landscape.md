### Slide 4: When You Hear a Competitor's Name on a Call
**Bullets:**
- Stay calm and professional — don't react negatively
- Say: "Oh great, they're good people" or "I've heard of them"
- Log the competitor name in your HubSpot call note under Intel Gathered
- Pivot to exploring whether they need a backup or overflow option
- Never ask "What don't you like about them?"

**Speaker Notes (EN):** This is a critical moment on any call. When someone says "We already use Better Box" or "Cardinal handles our contents," your instinct might be to compete. Don't. Instead, acknowledge it positively and pivot. Say something like: "That's great — they do good work. A lot of companies we work with like having a backup option for when their primary vendor is at capacity. Would it make sense to have us as a backup?" This approach is non-threatening and opens the door without burning anything down. And always log the competitor name — Matt uses this intel to understand the market.

**Speaker Notes (ES):** Este es un momento critico en cualquier llamada. Cuando alguien dice "Ya usamos Better Box" o "Cardinal maneja nuestros contenidos," tu instinto podria ser competir. No lo hagas. En cambio, reconocelo positivamente y cambia el enfoque. Di algo como: "Que bien — ellos hacen buen trabajo. Muchas empresas con las que trabajamos prefieren tener una opcion de respaldo para cuando su proveedor principal esta a capacidad. Tendria sentido tenernos como respaldo?" Este enfoque no es amenazante y abre la puerta sin quemar nada. Y siempre registra el nombre del competidor — Matt usa esta informacion para entender el mercado.

### Slide 5: The Backup/Overflow Angle
**Bullets:**
- Most restoration companies need more than one contents vendor
- Busy seasons (monsoon, holidays, fire season) overwhelm single vendors
- Adjusters get frustrated when their vendor can't respond quickly
- Being "Vendor #2" often leads to becoming Vendor #1 over time
- Frame it as: "We'd love to be in your rotation"

**Speaker Notes (EN):** The backup and overflow angle is your best friend. It's non-confrontational, it's true, and it works. During busy seasons — monsoon flooding, fire season, even just a random week when three water losses hit at once — their primary vendor might not be able to respond for 48 hours. That's when they call us. And once they see our quality of work, our documentation, our speed — we often move from backup to primary. So don't feel like "backup" is a lesser position. It's how you get your foot in the door.

**Speaker Notes (ES):** El angulo de respaldo y desbordamiento es tu mejor amigo. No es confrontacional, es verdad, y funciona. Durante las temporadas ocupadas — inundaciones de monzon, temporada de incendios, incluso una semana aleatoria cuando tres perdidas de agua llegan al mismo tiempo — su proveedor principal podria no poder responder por 48 horas. Ahi es cuando nos llaman. Y una vez que ven nuestra calidad de trabajo, nuestra documentacion, nuestra velocidad — frecuentemente pasamos de respaldo a primarios. Asi que no sientas que "respaldo" es una posicion menor. Es como metes el pie en la puerta.

### Slide 8: What NOT to Do — The Three Nevers
**Bullets:**
- **Never trash-talk a competitor** — "They're terrible" or "We hear bad things" is off-limits
- **Never compare pricing** — "We're cheaper than Cardinal" is a losing game
- **Never promise we're "better"** — Instead say "We'd be a great option for you"
- If they push you to compare, say: "I really can't speak to their process — I can only tell you about ours"

**Speaker Notes (EN):** These are hard rules, not guidelines. Never trash-talk. It makes you look unprofessional, it could get back to the competitor, and it actually makes the prospect trust you less. Never compare pricing — you don't even know their pricing, and competing on price is a race to the bottom. And never say "we're better." You don't know their situation, and it comes across as arrogant. If someone pushes you to compare, the perfect deflection is: "I really can't speak to their process — I can only tell you about ours. And what I can tell you is that we specialize in contents, we document everything through Encircle, and we'd love to be in your rotation."

**Speaker Notes (ES):** Estas son reglas estrictas, no sugerencias. Nunca hables mal. Te hace ver poco profesional, podria llegar al competidor, y realmente hace que el prospecto confie menos en ti. Nunca compares precios — ni siquiera sabes sus precios, y competir en precio es una carrera hacia el fondo. Y nunca digas "somos mejores." No conoces su situacion, y se ve arrogante. Si alguien te presiona para comparar, la desviacion perfecta es: "Realmente no puedo hablar sobre su proceso — solo puedo contarte sobre el nuestro. Y lo que puedo decirte es que nos especializamos en contenidos, documentamos todo a traves de Encircle, y nos encantaria estar en tu rotacion."

### Slide 9: Handling Common Objections About Competitors
**Bullets:**
- "We're happy with our current vendor" → "That's great! Would it make sense to have a backup for busy times?"
- "We've used [competitor] for years" → "That relationship is valuable. We'd love to be an additional resource."
- "Why should I switch?" → "I'm not asking you to switch — I'm offering a backup option."
- "Your competitor offered us a better deal" → "I appreciate that. Our value is in our specialization and documentation."

**Speaker Notes (EN):** These are the four objections you'll hear most often, and here's how to handle each one. Notice a pattern? You're never attacking their current relationship. You're positioning yourself alongside it, not instead of it. The "backup" framing takes all the pressure off the prospect. They don't have to make a big decision or break up with anyone. They just have to say "sure, keep us in mind." And that's all you need to get started.

**Speaker Notes (ES):** Estas son las cuatro objeciones que vas a escuchar con mas frecuencia, y asi es como manejar cada una. Notas un patron? Nunca estas atacando su relacion actual. Te estas posicionando al lado de ella, no en lugar de ella. El encuadre de "respaldo" quita toda la presion del prospecto. No tienen que tomar una decision grande o terminar con nadie. Solo tienen que decir "claro, tennos en mente." Y eso es todo lo que necesitas para empezar.

Note which competitor, how long the relationship has been, whether they seem happy or just stuck, and whether they're open to alternatives. Matt reviews these notes regularly and uses this data to understand where the opportunities are. If you notice that three adjusters this week mentioned that Cardinal is backed up, that's a signal. Your ability to gather and log this information is just as valuable as booking a meeting.

Registrala en tu nota de llamada en HubSpot bajo la seccion de Inteligencia Recopilada. Anota cual competidor, cuanto tiempo ha durado la relacion, si parecen contentos o solo atascados, y si estan abiertos a alternativas. ### Slide 12: Quiz / Reflection
**Scenario-Based Questions:**

1. An adjuster says "We already use Better Box for all our contents." What do you say?
2. A project manager asks "Why are you guys better than Cardinal?" How do you respond?
3. You hear that a competitor dropped the ball on a job. Do you mention this to the next prospect? Why or why not?
4. Name three ways 1-800-Packouts differentiates from general restoration companies.
5. What do you log in HubSpot when you hear a competitor's name?

**Speaker Notes (EN):** Let's test what you've learned. Take a minute to think through each scenario before answering. For question 1, remember the backup/overflow angle. For question 2, remember the deflection technique — you can't speak to their process, only ours. For question 3, the answer is always no — even if it's true, using someone else's failure to sell makes you look opportunistic, not professional. For question 4, think contents-only focus, Encircle documentation, insurance-approved, local owner-operated, and overflow capacity. And for question 5, log the competitor name, relationship length, satisfaction level, and openness to alternatives in your Intel Gathered section.

**Speaker Notes (ES):** Vamos a probar lo que has aprendido. Toma un minuto para pensar en cada escenario antes de responder. Para la pregunta 1, recuerda el angulo de respaldo/desbordamiento. Para la pregunta 2, recuerda la tecnica de desviacion — no puedes hablar de su proceso, solo del nuestro. Para la pregunta 3, la respuesta siempre es no — aunque sea verdad, usar el fracaso de alguien mas para vender te hace ver oportunista, no profesional. Para la pregunta 4, piensa en enfoque solo en contenidos, documentacion de Encircle, aprobado por seguros, operado por dueno local, y capacidad de desbordamiento. Y para la pregunta 5, registra el nombre del competidor, duracion de la relacion, nivel de satisfaccion, y apertura a alternativas en tu seccion de Inteligencia Recopilada.
