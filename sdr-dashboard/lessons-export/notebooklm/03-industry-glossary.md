# Industry Glossary

## Training Module for 1-800-Packouts SDR Onboarding

This is a complete glossary of every term an SDR at 1-800-Packouts will encounter. These words come up in every call, every HubSpot note, and every conversation with Matt. Knowing these terms is the difference between sounding like an industry professional and sounding like someone reading a script.

---

## Everyday Terms

Contents: Everything inside a home — furniture, clothes, electronics, personal items. The opposite of "structure."

Structure: The building itself — walls, floors, roof, framing. 1-800-Packouts does not do structure work.

Packout: The process of packing and removing contents from a damaged home.

Pack-back: Returning contents to the home after repairs are complete.

Mitigation: Emergency response to stop damage from getting worse — water extraction, board-up, tarping. This is done by restoration companies, not by 1-800-Packouts.

Restoration: Repairing the structure back to pre-loss condition. Done by general contractors and restoration companies.

Loss: The event that caused damage — fire, water, storm. Example: "The loss occurred on January 15th."

Claim: The insurance case. Each loss equals one claim with a unique claim number.

Scope (of work): The detailed list of what needs to be done and what insurance will pay for.

## Insurance and Estimating Terms

Xactimate: Industry-standard estimating software. Every estimate in the contents restoration world is written in Xactimate.

RCV (Replacement Cost Value): Full cost to replace an item new. The "retail price."

ACV (Actual Cash Value): RCV minus depreciation. What insurance pays upfront.

Depreciation: Reduction in value for age and wear. Insurance withholds this initially and releases it when work is completed.

Supplement: Additional scope submitted after the original estimate, when more work is needed than originally anticipated. Very common in this industry.

Line item: One row in a Xactimate estimate. Example: "Pack medium box — qty 45."

CPS code: The Xactimate category/product/subcategory code for a line item.

O&P (Overhead and Profit): 10% overhead plus 10% profit that contractors add on top of Xactimate pricing. Standard across the industry.

## People and Roles

Adjuster: Insurance company employee or contractor who inspects damage and approves the scope. The gatekeeper for vendor selection.

Staff adjuster: Full-time employee of one insurance carrier.

Independent adjuster (IA): Contractor who handles claims for multiple carriers.

Public adjuster (PA): Hired by the homeowner, not the carrier, to negotiate a better settlement. Less common.

GC (General Contractor): Oversees the rebuild of the structure. Often subcontracts the packout to companies like 1-800-Packouts.

Restoration company: Specializes in fire and water damage repair. Sometimes handles packout in-house, sometimes subcontracts it.

Property manager (PM): Manages rental properties, apartment complexes, HOAs, or commercial properties. Calls a packout company when a unit has a loss.

TPA (Third-Party Administrator): Companies that manage claims on behalf of insurance carriers. They may maintain their own vendor lists.

## 1-800-Packouts Specific Terms

Fire lead: A homeowner who just had a structure fire. The highest priority lead type. Source: fireleads.com.

azfirehelp.com: The homeowner-facing resource website for 1-800-Packouts. The SDR texts this link to every fire lead after the first call or voicemail.

Sales line: (623) 300-2119. This is the number used for all outbound calls and the number on all marketing materials.

Daily summary: The end-of-day report sent to Matt via Google Chat. The template is in the Playbook section of the dashboard.

Escalation: When a lead is hot enough for Matt to take over. Escalate via Google Chat or phone call, immediately.

## Key Takeaways

1. Know the difference between contents and structure — we do contents only
2. Understanding insurance terms (RCV, ACV, supplement) lets you speak intelligently with adjusters
3. Know the roles — adjuster, GC, restoration company, PM — because each requires a different approach
4. Fire leads are Priority 1, always
