# The Fire Leads Program

## Training Module for 1-800-Packouts SDR Onboarding

This document explains how the fire leads program works — from alert to close. Fire leads are the highest-priority, most time-sensitive leads. Understanding this workflow is critical because speed wins in this business.

---

## What Is fireleads.com?

fireleads.com is a service that monitors fire department dispatch data in Maricopa and Pima counties (the Phoenix and Tucson metro areas). When a structure fire is reported, the service sends a real-time alert with:

- Address of the fire
- Owner name and phone number (from public records)
- Incident details (date, time, type of fire)
- Property information (home value, square footage when available)

These alerts arrive via Gmail (in the Fire Leads label) and Google Chat (in the fire leads space). The SDR checks both every morning as the first task of the day.

## Why Fire Leads Are Priority 1

Fire leads are the highest-value, most time-sensitive leads because:

1. Speed wins. The first packout company to reach a homeowner after a fire usually gets the job. Most homeowners do not know what packout is — the first person to explain it and offer help wins their trust.

2. Direct-to-homeowner. No gatekeeper. No vendor list. No RFP. The homeowner chooses who packs their stuff, and insurance almost always approves.

3. High emotional state. These people are in crisis. A calm, empathetic call from someone who can actually help stands out against the chaos they are experiencing.

4. Less competition. Better Box and Cardinal do not have the same fire lead monitoring. If we call first, we are often the only packout company the homeowner has heard from.

## The Fire Lead Workflow

Here is the exact step-by-step flow:

Step 1: Alert arrives via Gmail and Google Chat.

Step 2: Call the next morning by noon. Even if the fire was at 2 AM, the SDR calls the next morning. Work starts at 8 AM — fire leads are the first thing checked.

Step 3: If the homeowner answers, follow Script 1 (Fire Lead — Homeowner Call). Lead with empathy: "Are you and your family safe?" Explain what we do: pack, clean, store, return — all covered by insurance. Get: insurance carrier, adjuster name, competitor check, best callback number.

Step 4: If voicemail, leave the VM script (keep it under 30 seconds).

Step 5: After the call (whether live conversation or voicemail), ALWAYS send the text message template. This always includes the azfirehelp.com link. Every time, no exceptions.

Step 6: Log in HubSpot. Create a note using the full template: Call Type (Fire Lead), Outcome, Contact, Intel Gathered, Next Steps.

Step 7: If the lead is HOT (ready to schedule, has insurance, no competitor), escalate to Matt IMMEDIATELY via Google Chat or phone call.

Step 8: Follow up in 2 days if no response. Maximum 3 attempts over 7 days.

## azfirehelp.com

azfirehelp.com is the homeowner-facing resource website. It is designed for people who just had a fire and need help understanding what to do next.

The site includes:
- Fire damage insurance claim guide
- Fire recovery checklist and timeline
- "Who does what after a fire" explainer
- Free recovery tools

When to send it: After EVERY first call or voicemail to a fire lead, send this text:

"Hi [NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] from 1-800-Packouts. I just tried calling about the fire at your home. We help families protect and store their belongings during repairs — fully covered by insurance. Feel free to call or text us anytime. Here's some info: azfirehelp.com"

This gives the homeowner something tangible to look at, builds credibility, and keeps the company top of mind.

## Common Fire Lead Scenarios

"How did you get my number?"
Response: "We work closely with local fire departments and monitor fire incidents in the area so we can offer help to families who need it. We're an insurance-approved vendor — we don't charge you anything directly."

They already have a packout company:
Response: "No problem at all. Who are you working with? Great. If anything changes or you need a second opinion, we're always available." Log the competitor name. Do not push.

They have not filed a claim yet:
Response: "That's totally okay. The first step is usually calling your insurance company to file a claim. Once you have an adjuster assigned, that's when the packout conversation usually happens. I'll follow up in a few days to see where things stand."

They are emotional or upset:
Let them talk. Do not rush. "I'm so sorry you're going through this." The SDR's job is to be the calm, helpful voice in their chaos. The sale takes care of itself when they trust you.

## Key Takeaways

1. Fire leads are ALWAYS Priority 1 — check first thing every morning
2. Call by noon the next morning — speed wins
3. Always send the text with azfirehelp.com after every first call or voicemail
4. Log everything in HubSpot using the full note template
5. Escalate hot leads to Matt immediately
6. Follow up: 3 attempts over 7 days, then move on
7. Empathy first. You are not selling — you are helping.
