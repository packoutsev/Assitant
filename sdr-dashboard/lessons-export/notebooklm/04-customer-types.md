# Who You're Calling & Why — The 4 Customer Types

## Training Module for 1-800-Packouts SDR Onboarding

This document explains the four types of people an SDR calls, why each matters, and how to approach them. There are 4 call scripts because these are 4 very different conversations. Understanding WHO you are talking to is as important as what you say.

---

## Customer Type 1: Homeowners (Fire/Water Leads) — Priority 1

Who they are: Regular people who just had a disaster. Their house caught fire, a pipe burst, or a water heater failed. They are stressed, scared, probably displaced, and overwhelmed with insurance paperwork.

Why they matter: This is the direct-to-consumer channel. When a homeowner chooses 1-800-Packouts, the adjuster almost always approves it. Homeowner choice is powerful.

Your tone: Empathetic, calm, helpful. You are NOT selling. You are a concerned professional offering a lifeline. "I heard about the fire at your home and wanted to see how you're doing."

What you need from them:
- Are they safe?
- Insurance carrier name
- Adjuster name (if assigned)
- Has anyone else contacted them about packout?
- Best callback number and time

Key insight: Many homeowners do not know packout exists. They think their stuff is just gone. When you explain that we pack, clean, store, and return their belongings — and insurance covers it — it is genuinely life-changing news for them.

Script to use: Script 1 (Fire Lead — Homeowner Call)

## Customer Type 2: GCs and Restoration Companies — Priority 3

Who they are: Businesses that do fire and water restoration work. They get the job first (mitigation plus rebuild) and need someone to handle the contents. Some do packout in-house; many subcontract it.

Why they matter: One good GC relationship equals a steady stream of jobs. They do multiple restoration projects per month. If they sub all their packout work to 1-800-Packouts, that is recurring business without needing to find individual homeowners.

Your tone: Business-to-business, professional, peer-level. You are one vendor talking to another. No hard sell — just exploring whether there is a fit.

What you need from them:
- Do they handle packout in-house or sub it out?
- Who is their current packout vendor?
- How long have they been with them? Contract or job-by-job?
- Volume — how many packout jobs per month?
- Who is the decision maker for packout referrals?
- Any overflow or backup needs?

Key insight: Even if they are happy with their current vendor, most GCs want a backup. "Would it make sense to have us in your back pocket?" is a powerful low-pressure close.

Script to use: Script 2 (Cold Outreach — GC/Restoration)

## Customer Type 3: Insurance Adjusters — Priority 4

Who they are: The people at insurance companies who inspect damage, write scopes, and approve vendors. They handle dozens of claims per month.

Why they matter: An adjuster who trusts 1-800-Packouts will recommend us to every homeowner on every claim they handle. One adjuster relationship can generate 5 to 10 jobs per year.

Your tone: Professional, respectful of their time, knowledgeable. They deal with vendors all day. You need to sound like you know the industry.

What you need from them:
- What carrier are they with?
- Do they handle property claims (fire, water)?
- How does their vendor referral process work?
- Do they have a preferred vendor list?
- Who manages the vendor list?
- How many contents claims per month?

Key insight: This is the LONGEST sales cycle. Adjusters do not switch vendors quickly. But getting on a preferred vendor list is extremely valuable. Matt handles all adjuster relationship closings — the SDR's job is to get the introduction.

Script to use: Script 3 (Cold Outreach — Adjuster)

## Customer Type 4: Property Managers — Priority 5

Who they are: Companies that manage apartment complexes, HOAs, commercial properties, or rental homes. When a unit has a fire or water loss, they need emergency packout.

Why they matter: They manage hundreds or thousands of units. Even if losses are infrequent, when they happen, the PM needs someone reliable RIGHT NOW. Being their go-to vendor is a low-frequency, high-reliability play.

Your tone: Solution-oriented, preparedness-focused. "Do you have a vendor in place for when this happens?" Most do not.

What you need from them:
- Portfolio size (how many units?)
- Recent fire or water losses?
- Current packout vendor (if any)
- Who makes the vendor decision — them or insurance?
- Interest in having 1-800-Packouts on file for emergencies

Key insight: Many property managers have never thought about packout until they need it. You are not competing with another vendor — you are filling a gap they did not know existed.

Script to use: Script 4 (Cold Outreach — Property Manager)

## Key Takeaways

1. Four customer types, four scripts, four different tones
2. Homeowners (fire leads) are always Priority 1 — speed and empathy win
3. GCs are about backup positioning and recurring relationships
4. Adjusters are long-cycle but high-value — get the intro, Matt closes
5. Property managers are about preparedness — fill the gap they do not know they have
6. Always escalate to Matt when there is real interest. You discover and qualify. Matt closes.
