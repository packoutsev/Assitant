# The Competitive Landscape

## Training Module for 1-800-Packouts SDR Onboarding

This document covers who else does contents packout in the Phoenix metro area, how to handle hearing competitor names on calls, and what makes 1-800-Packouts different. The SDR will never trash-talk a competitor or compare pricing — this is about understanding the landscape.

---

## Who Else Does This in the Valley

The Phoenix metro area has a handful of companies that do contents packout. You will hear their names on calls. Here is what to know.

### Better Box
Contents packout company operating in the Valley. This is the most direct competitor for the same type of work. You will hear their name from GCs and adjusters frequently.

When you hear their name on a call:
- Log it: "Current vendor: Better Box"
- Do not trash-talk: "They're a good company. We just like to make sure you have options."
- Ask: "How long have you been with them? Contract or job-by-job?"
- Plant the seed: "A lot of companies keep a backup vendor. Would it make sense for Matt to introduce himself?"

### Cardinal
Contents and restoration competitor in the Valley. Sometimes handles both structure and contents, which can be an advantage (one vendor for everything) or a disadvantage (less specialized in contents).

When you hear their name: Same approach — log it, do not trash-talk, explore overflow and backup opportunities.

## How 1-800-Packouts Differentiates

When someone asks "why should I use you guys?" — do not answer that directly. Escalate pricing and partnership questions to Matt. But here is what makes the company different, so you understand the value:

- Contents-only focus: We do not do structure work. Contents is all we do. That means more specialized crews, better processes, and more attention to belongings.
- Insurance-approved: We work directly with insurance companies. No out-of-pocket cost for homeowners.
- Documentation: We use Encircle for detailed photo documentation of every item. This helps with the insurance claim.
- Local, owner-operated: Matt Roumain owns and operates the business. When Matt says he will handle something, he means it personally. GCs and adjusters work with Matt directly, not a call center.
- Capacity: The company is actively building capacity to handle overflow work, which matters to GCs who sometimes cannot get their primary vendor fast enough.

## What NOT to Do

- Never trash-talk a competitor. Ever. The restoration world is small and people talk.
- Never compare pricing. You do not know your company's pricing and you do not know the competitor's pricing. Escalate to Matt.
- Never promise the company is "better." Just position 1-800-Packouts as an option, a backup, or an alternative.
- If they are happy with their current vendor: Log it and move on. "No problem at all. If anything changes, we're here."

## Key Takeaways

1. Know the competitors (Better Box, Cardinal) so you are not caught off guard
2. Never trash-talk — log the competitor name and explore backup/overflow angles
3. Understand what makes 1-800-Packouts different (contents-only, local, owner-operated)
4. Never discuss or compare pricing — escalate to Matt
5. If they are happy with their vendor, that is fine. Log and move on.
