# What Is Contents Packout?

## Training Module for 1-800-Packouts SDR Onboarding

This document explains what contents packout is, why it matters, and how 1-800-Packouts fits into the insurance restoration industry. This is foundational knowledge for anyone making sales calls in this space.

---

## The Big Picture

When a home has a fire, flood, or water damage, two things need to happen:

1. The structure (walls, floors, roof) needs to be repaired or rebuilt. This is called "restoration" or "mitigation" and is handled by general contractors and restoration companies.

2. The contents (furniture, clothes, electronics, family photos, kitchenware — everything inside the home) need to be protected. This is where 1-800-Packouts comes in.

1-800-Packouts does not fix houses. We protect everything inside them.

## The 4-Phase Process

Every job follows the same cycle:

### Phase 1 — Packout
The crew goes to the home and carefully packs up all salvageable belongings. This is not "throwing stuff in boxes." Every item is inventoried, photographed, and packed with specialized materials. Think: moving company meets insurance documentation.

### Phase 2 — Cleaning
Smoke-damaged clothes, soot-covered electronics, water-soaked furniture — these are cleaned using specialized processes. Ultrasonic cleaning, ozone treatment, dry cleaning, hand cleaning. Not everything is salvageable, but a lot more can be saved than homeowners expect.

### Phase 3 — Storage
While the home is being rebuilt (which can take 3 to 12 months), all contents are stored in a climate-controlled facility. Contents are stored by job — the homeowner's belongings stay together, labeled, and accessible.

### Phase 4 — Pack-Back
Once the home is repaired, everything is brought back and put where it belongs. Furniture placed, boxes unpacked, pictures hung. The homeowner walks into a home that looks like it did before the loss, or better.

## Why This Matters to Homeowners

People who just had a fire are in crisis. They are displaced, stressed, dealing with insurance, and terrified about losing their belongings — family photos, heirlooms, children's things. Many don't even know that contents packout is a service that exists, or that their insurance covers it.

When you call a fire lead, you are not selling. You are offering help that most people desperately need but don't know exists. That is why empathy comes first in every call.

## Why This Matters Financially

Every packout job is paid for by the homeowner's insurance company. The homeowner has zero out-of-pocket cost. That is one of the most powerful things you can say on a call.

The main components of every job are:
- Packing labor — crews carefully pack and inventory every item
- Storage — monthly fees while the home is being rebuilt
- Cleaning — specialized processes for smoke, soot, and water damage
- Pack-back — returning everything to the home after repairs

You will never discuss pricing with anyone — that is the owner Matt's role. But knowing that insurance covers everything lets you confidently tell homeowners: "There's no cost to you."

## Key Vocabulary

These terms come up constantly:

- Contents: Everything inside the home (as opposed to "structure" which is the building itself)
- Packout: The process of packing and removing contents
- Pack-back: Returning contents to the home after repairs
- Salvageable: Can be cleaned and restored to pre-loss condition
- Non-salvageable: Too damaged to save; will be listed on the insurance claim as a loss
- Inventory: The documented list of every item packed (photos, descriptions, condition)
- Job / Claim: Used interchangeably. Each customer situation is a "job" for the company and a "claim" for insurance.

## Key Takeaways

1. 1-800-Packouts protects the contents inside damaged homes — we do not do structure work
2. The process has 4 phases: Packout → Cleaning → Storage → Pack-back
3. Insurance pays for everything — zero out-of-pocket cost for the homeowner
4. Homeowners in crisis need empathy first, information second
5. Never discuss pricing — that is Matt's role
